Project ARCHETYPE

Overview

Modern design meets minimalist - ARCHETYPE offers a clean and simple experience for content creators to collect, organize, collaborate, create, and share their work with other creatives and fellow followers.

Role: UX Design, Visual Design, Brand Identity

Tool Box: Figma, InVision, Adobe Creative Suite, Xtension, Lucid Chart

Deliverables: User Surveys, Competitive Analysis, User Personas, User Stories & Flows, Prototyping & Testing.

Problem

To date, there are several cloud storage platforms available to consumers, most of which try to cover a wide audience. What about a niche crowd of content creators? How could a platform offer all in one services for organizations, content creation, and collaboration?

Solution

ARCHETYPE aims to solve that problem by streamlining these services into one main platform. It will allow users to create and organize content, collaborate with other creatives, and share their work with their followers.

Market & User Research

The cloud storage space has been around for several years and is an established business, used by millions on a global scale. Upon looking at the possibility of a new market entrant, I needed to first identify who were our competitors and understand the market. A SWOT Analysis was used to help initiate the process.

Competitive Analysis

Google Drive, Microsoft One Drive, and DropBox were targeted as key competitors in the market. Through the SWOT Analysis, I was able to identify certain weaknesses and opportunities for a new market entrant.

  • Google Drive has a strong presence in the market through its already existing userbase of other services. However, the features are basic and rather limited.
  • MS One Drive has a very strong UI with a clean interface. However, features and services are targeting the enterprise and family spaces.
  • DropBox has been around for quite some time, however most of their market targeting are more towards the enterprise and business sector.

User Surveys

To gather more insight into the cloud storage space and its consumers, an understanding of user behavior and patterns were needed.

In order to accomplish this, I created a simple user survey that focused on these key areas:

  • Ability to collect, organize, collaborate, create, and share content
  • Familiarity of cloud storage services
  • Consistency and frequency of usage
  • Pain points and frustrations

Key Insights

The survey data revealed that the market was well versed in the uses of cloud storage platforms. Many subscribers engaged these platforms for professional and personal uses such as content creation and collaboration.

And although subscription fees were listed as one of the frustrations, other issues like security features, user experience for 1st time users, and accessibility / inconsistencies of the service from one device to another were a few of the pain-points illustrated from the survey results.

User Personas

Using the data gained from the surveys, user personas were then created to assist with identifying the most common and basic functions needed from a new cloud storage platform.

The primary goal was to grow an understanding of potential users' adoption of a new service. And in order to address this, two user personas were created - the Visual Creator and the Content Collaborator.

User Flows

Using the most important features gained from the stories and combining that with the data from the personas, I then began transferring the information into userflows. The flows would serve as a visual blueprint of how all the needs and features would integrate into a functioning product.

Wireframes & Prototype

Referencing the user flows as a guide, I then began sketching out the basic wireframes that would serve as a visual aid of how the application could operate.

Once the sketches were complete, I then used Figma to replicate the sketches so as to transform them into a Lo-Fi clickable prototype for user testing.

Lo-Fi Testing

After the initial wireframes were finished, I then imported them to InVission where they were put together to construct a Lo-Fi clickable prototype. User testing was conducted both in-person and remote through facetime. Feedback was provided during this period which later led to the following refinements.

Refinements:

  • Fix alignment of call to action buttons within the user dashboard field
  • Buttons need to be easily presentable to the user
  • Fix typography for layout in the user dashboard field
  • Address spacing of buttons and typography throughout entire layout of design

Branding

Given that this industry was already a heavily saturated market, I had to identify a point of entry to make a splash large enough for consumers to notice. In order to achieve this, the product's branding needed to evoke a sense of purpose that also resonated with the brand's services - something to stoke the interest of the community.

Through the help of some mind-mapping techniques and several sketches of what the product should convey, ARCHETYPE stood out with the most potential of what the brand would stand for.

The meaning behind ARCHETYPE is better expressed as the original pattern or model of which all things of the same type are a respresentation of the original source. It will stand for ideas that are attractive yet simple with a creative personality and can be accessible to any professional content creator.

Logo

For the logo portion, I knew I wanted it to represent something that was synonymous of what ARCHETYPE stood for.

I set out to work pouring through numerous sketches of what a potential logo could be, but none seemed to have captured my attention. So rather than focusing on a mark, I redirected my focus on the name itself - a word that is hardly used or heard in an everyday conversation and is unique enough to evoke a sense of curiousity regarding the brand's character and personality.

Typography

Two typefaces were chosen for this brand that were intended to represent a strong charisma that is associated with the mood of what defines the brand

Brand Typeface

Montserrat is the primary typeface of ARCHETYPE which is mainly used for headlines and displays. The typeface comes in a number of variations in weights and sizes

Complimentary Typeface

Lato is the second typeface that will be used for subtitles and body text.

Colors

Three layers for the color palette were chosen, each representing a certain identity that would be expressed throughout the brand's overall look and character.

  • The primary color is turqouise which is meant to signify professionalism and creativity and is also a great compliment to the various shades of gray and white colors.
  • Black illustrates the strength of the brand and can be seen in text, headings, and throughout the background.
  • Vermillion red adds vibrance and determination, while mustard yellow brings with it a splash of clarity and energy.

Once the last piece of the puzzle was identified, I then began putting togehter a cohesive style guide that would serve as a blueprint to reference the brand's overall design patterns and preserve the brand's identity.

Hi-Fi Prototype

Once the style guide was completed, I circled back to the original wireframes and mockups used for the low-fidelity prototype and began designing the high-fidelity prototype.

During this process, several iterations and revisions were made based on preference testing among a small group of participants. This was to ensure that the best and well qualified designs were focused on and presented in the final product.

Hi-Fi Testing

User testing was conducted in-person and also in tandem with the revision and iteration process. The reason behind these on-going user tests was to identify if there were any other problems or issues with the prototype, alongside gathering additional user-based input that would provide invaluable insight into the site's experience and usability.

Some of the feedback expressed during the testing period included:

  • Font adjustments
  • Inconsistency of button placements
  • Modifications of color use to give the site an overall consistent look of a clean and simple application

Adjusted and repositioned upload button, changed color of side tab to match status bar, and realigned typography for a more tighter and cleaner look.

Conclusion

Takeaways

The undertaking of this project was definitely a challenge, but it was also fun. During the entire process, I researched and questioned certain areas that pushed my mental boundaries in creation and ideation. And as I found out early on in the process, designing something from scratch is not easy. There is a lot of time and effort that goes into this phase before pencil meets paper in drawing out the 1st batch of sketches.

Reflection

Ideas will always shift, designs will always revise and iterate upon themselves, there will always be room for additional testing on prototypes and no matter how many times a design is made final, there is almost always a certainty that there will be several other finals before a final design is truly agreed upon. Learning will always remain as a constant variable in UX Design and the value achieved from growth is priceless.
